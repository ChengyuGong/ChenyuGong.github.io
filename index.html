Vector Databases - HPDIC lab

Advisor: Prof. Dongfang Zhao

In my recent research project, I tackled a critical challenge in scientific data management by developing the Order-Preserving Dimension Reduction (OPDR) method to efficiently process k-nearest neighbors queries in multimodal datasets. This method significantly reduces the dimensionality of embedding vectors while maintaining accurate relational data in a reduced-dimensional space. I successfully formulated a quantitative function that elucidates the correlation between reduced dimensions and other key variables, culminating in a preprint. Our experiments verified that OPDR effectively manages high-dimensional data, enhancing performance in time-sensitive scientific applications.

I am currently exploring the application of our developed OPDR algorithm in the financial sector. Additionally, our lab's upcoming research will focus on investigating the applications of Entropic Multimodality in vector databases. This innovative research initiative aims to leverage the principles of Entropic Multimodality to enhance the processing efficiency and accuracy of vector databases during complex query execution and data interactions.
